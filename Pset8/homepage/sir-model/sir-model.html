SIR Model

An SIR model is a simple mathematical model used to simulate the spread of diseses amongst a population. Susceptible (S) repersents the population that has not yet been infected and as such is vulnerable to becoming infected. Infected (I) is the section of the population currently infected with the disease. Infected indivisuals have a chance to infect other susceptible people depending on the disease infection rate. Recovered (R) is the section of population that has recovered from the disease and developed immunity as such they can no longer infect or be infected by other people.

Total Population: the total population at the beginning of the simulation. The sum of susceptible, infected and recovered people will always equal this number.

Infected Start: the starting population of infected indivisuals at the beginning of the simulation.

Infectious Rate: the rate at which infected indivisuals spread the disease to those susceptible each day.

Recovery Rate: the rate at which infected indivisuals recover from the disease each day.
